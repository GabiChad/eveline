Eveline Kurylak

I want to investigate the feeling aspect as a social artistic project. My goal is to share the tranquility I find during the process of intuitively shifting residual forms into a 'Healing Practice'.

Wanting to further deepen a subconscious symbolic language that is part of this, so also in the elaboration phase the participant performs a ritual in response to my visual language to undergo a personal transition phase. This no longer applies so explicitly in our current society. (Carl Jung's work would be a great source of inspiration in this research.)

WHY

My origins are a melting pot of cultures that could arise from Limburg's mining history, so I am Ukrainian, Greek and Canarian. You can describe me as a caring person with a fascination for psychoanalysis, the subconscious and the intuitive. In recent years I have examined my transgenerational trauma and became aware of this and to be conscious about it. I graduated as a graphic designer in Hasselt in 2016. During my graphic training I discovered my love for collages. The result is achieved by taking existing images out of context and being guided by coincidences during the working process. I create my personal visual language from the subconscious and collected images. The absent is present by using residual forms as the main component. Looking for connections between shapes, collected images and my past. Holding an impression, then adding a piece of myself to it, letting it go and blending it with what presents itself. Themes I explore are the subconscious and the symbolic. Finding my way as a visual artist, graphic designer and feeling person. Based on the collage technique, the result can be two-dimensional, digital, moving or a scenography, preferably all these different versions mixed together


HOW

I want to apply different techniques to create a multi-dimensional space. The scenography is decisive where video, audio, textiles, collage, text, etc. are brought together into a formal theater in which the participant is immersed. Let this season continue at the rhythm of nature, looking back at the legacy and symbolic language of those who have gone before us and lived in harmony with nature. In this way, connecting the present with the past.
